The 2×2×2 Model

2×2×2モデル

📐

Throughout this unit we use a simplified general equilibrium model with:
2 Inputs: Labor (L) and Capital (K)  |  2 Goods: X and Y  |  2 Consumers: A and B

本ユニット全体で以下の簡略化された一般均衡モデルを使用：
2生産要素：労働(L)・資本(K)  |  2財：X・Y  |  2消費者：A・B

1

Production: Isoquant Curves & MRTS

生産：等産量曲線とMRTS

Isoquant Curve (IQ)

等産量曲線（IQ）

The locus of all input combinations (L, K) that produce the same quantity of output. Higher IQs represent higher output levels. Convex to origin.

同一の産出量を達成するすべての投入物の組み合わせの軌跡。より上位のIQはより高い産出水準を示す。原点に向かって凸型。

MRTSLK — Marginal Rate of Technical Substitution

MRTSLK — 限界技術代替率

The number of units of K that can be given up in exchange for one additional unit of L while keeping output constant. Equals the slope of the IQ (negative).

産出量を一定に保ちながら、Lを1単位増加させる代わりに放棄できるKの単位数。IQの傾き（マイナス）に等しい。

Key Formula
MRTSLK = −ΔK/ΔL = MPL / MPK
where MPL = ∂Q/∂L (Marginal Product of Labor) | MPK = ∂Q/∂K (Marginal Product of Capital)
2

Consumption: Indifference Curves & MRS

消費：無差別曲線とMRS

Indifference Curve (IC)

無差別曲線（IC）

The locus of all consumption bundles (X, Y) that give the consumer the same utility. Higher ICs represent higher utility. Convex to origin (diminishing MRS).

消費者に同一の効用を与えるすべての消費束の軌跡。上位ほど高い効用。原点に向かって凸型（限界代替率逓減）。

MRSXY — Marginal Rate of Substitution

MRSXY — 限界代替率

The rate at which a consumer is willing to trade Y for X while maintaining the same utility. Equals the slope of the IC (negative).

効用を一定に保ちながら、XとYを交換する比率。ICの傾き（マイナス）に等しい。

Key Formula
MRSXY = −ΔY/ΔX = MUX / MUY
where MUX = ∂U/∂X (Marginal Utility of X) | MUY = ∂U/∂Y (Marginal Utility of Y)
3

Three Conditions for Pareto Optimality

パレート最適の3条件

Pareto Optimality: An allocation where it is impossible to make any one person better off without making someone else worse off.

パレート最適：誰かを悪化させることなく、誰かをより良くすることが不可能な資源配分。

(a) Production Efficiency生産効率性

Also called the Marginal Condition for Factor Substitution. Achieved when it is impossible to reallocate inputs (L, K) between goods X and Y to increase one output without decreasing the other.

「要素代替の限界条件」とも呼ばれる。一方の産出物を減らさずに他方を増やせない状態。

Condition (a)
MRTSXLK = MRTSYLK
The MRTS must be equal across all production activities → Points on the Production Contract Curve

(b) Consumption Efficiency消費効率性

Also called the Marginal Condition for Exchange. Achieved when it is impossible to redistribute X and Y between A and B to make one person better off without harming the other.

「交換の限界条件」とも呼ばれる。一方を悪化させずに他方を改善できない状態。

Condition (b)
MRSAXY = MRSBXY
The MRS must be equal for all consumers → Points on the Consumption Contract Curve

(c) Production-Consumption Efficiency生産＝消費効率性

Also called the Marginal Condition for Product Substitution. Links the production side (PPF/MRT) with the consumption side (IC/MRS).

「生産物代替の限界条件」とも呼ばれる。生産側（PPF/MRT）と消費側（IC/MRS）を結びつける。

Condition (c) — The Key Pareto Condition
MRSXY = MRTXY
MRTXY = slope of PPF = MCX/MCY  |  MRSXY = slope of IC = MUX/MUY

Summary: Three Pareto Conditions

まとめ：パレート最適の3条件

  • (a) Production Efficiency: MRTSXLK = MRTSYLK — inputs are efficiently allocated between goods
  • (a) 生産効率性： MRTSXLK = MRTSYLK — 生産要素が財間で効率的に配分
  • (b) Consumption Efficiency: MRSAXY = MRSBXY — goods are efficiently distributed between consumers
  • (b) 消費効率性： MRSAXY = MRSBXY — 財が消費者間で効率的に分配
  • (c) Production-Consumption Efficiency: MRSXY = MRTXY — production mix matches consumer preferences
  • (c) 生産＝消費効率性： MRSXY = MRTXY — 生産の組み合わせが消費者の選好に一致
4

The Two Fundamental Welfare Theorems

2つの基本的厚生定理

🥇 First Welfare Theorem第1厚生定理

A perfectly competitive equilibrium guarantees Pareto optimality. That is, conditions (a), (b), and (c) are all satisfied at a competitive equilibrium.

完全競争均衡はパレート最適を保証する。すなわち、条件(a)(b)(c)はすべて競争均衡で満たされる。

Why condition (a)?なぜ条件(a)か？
Firms minimize cost: w/r = MRTSLK → same for all firms → MRTSX=MRTSY

Why condition (b)?なぜ条件(b)か？
Consumers maximize utility: PX/PY = MRSXY → same for all consumers → MRSA=MRSB

Why condition (c)?なぜ条件(c)か？
Profit maximization: P=MC → PX/PY = MCX/MCY = MRTXY = MRSXY

🥈 Second Welfare Theorem第2厚生定理

Any Pareto-efficient allocation can be achieved as a competitive equilibrium — given a suitable redistribution of initial endowments.

適切な初期賦存量の再分配を行えば、あらゆるパレート効率的な配分を競争均衡として実現できる。

⚠️

Limitation: Redistribution requires lump-sum transfers (taxes/subsidies) that are in practice difficult and costly. Also, the "right" distribution requires value judgments about equity — which the second theorem alone cannot determine.

限界：再分配には一括移転（税・補助金）が必要だが、実際は困難・費用大。また「望ましい」分配は公平性についての価値判断を要する。

Q1 The Marginal Rate of Technical Substitution (MRTSLK) equals:限界技術代替率(MRTSLK)は何に等しいか？

A MUX / MUY
B MPL / MPK
C MCX / MCY
D PX / PY
MPL/MPK — MRTSLK = −ΔK/ΔL = MPL/MPK. It represents how much capital K can be replaced by labor L while keeping output constant, reflecting the relative productivities of inputs.MRTSLK = −ΔK/ΔL = MPL/MPK。産出量一定でLとKを代替する比率で、投入物の相対的生産性を反映。

Q2 Production efficiency (Pareto condition a) requires:生産効率性（パレート条件a）の要件は？

A MRSA = MRSB
B MRS = MRT
C MRTSXLK = MRTSYLK
D MPL = MPK
MRTSXLK = MRTSYLK — Condition (a) requires that the rate at which L and K can be substituted is equal in the production of X and Y. If not, output could be increased by reallocating inputs.条件(a)はXとYの生産でLとKの代替率が等しいことを要求。等しくなければ投入物の再配分で産出を増加できる。

Q3 The key production-consumption efficiency condition (Pareto condition c) states:生産消費効率性（パレート条件c）は？

A MRSXY = MRTXY
B MRTSX = MRTSY
C MRSA = MRSB
D MCX = MCY
MRSXY = MRTXY — This is the grand condition linking consumption (MRS = rate consumers are willing to trade X for Y) with production (MRT = rate the economy can transform X into Y). It's satisfied at the tangency of an IC with the PPF.消費（MRS=消費者がXとYを交換する意思比率）と生産（MRT=経済がXをYに転換する比率）を結ぶ大条件。ICとPPFの接点で満たされる。

Q4 What does the First Fundamental Theorem of Welfare Economics state?第1基本厚生定理の主張は？

A A perfectly competitive equilibrium achieves Pareto optimality完全競争均衡はパレート最適を達成する
B Any Pareto-optimal allocation can be achieved with redistributionあらゆるパレート最適配分は再分配で達成できる
C Government intervention always improves efficiency政府介入は常に効率性を改善する
D Social welfare is maximized when inequality is minimized不平等が最小化される時、社会厚生は最大化される
A perfectly competitive equilibrium achieves Pareto optimality完全競争均衡はパレート最適を達成 — This formalizes Adam Smith's "invisible hand." The decentralized price mechanism, without any central planning, leads to all three Pareto conditions being satisfied simultaneously.アダム・スミスの「見えざる手」を形式化。分散した価格メカニズムにより、中央計画なしに3つのパレート条件が同時に満たされる。

Q5 In an Edgeworth Box, the Contract Curve is:エッジワースボックスにおける契約曲線とは？

A A straight line from one origin to the other一方の原点から他方への直線
B The locus of all tangency points of the two sets of IQ (or IC) curves2組のIQ（またはIC）曲線の接点の軌跡
C The boundary of the boxボックスの境界
D The PPF of the economy経済のPPF
Locus of tangency points接点の軌跡 — At each point on the Contract Curve, the IQs (in production box) or ICs (in consumption box) of the two firms/consumers are tangent. This means MRTSX=MRTSY or MRSA=MRSB — Pareto efficient.契約曲線の各点で2つのIQ（生産）またはIC（消費）が接する。MRTSX=MRTSYまたはMRSA=MRSBが成立 — パレート効率的。

Q6 The Second Welfare Theorem implies that equity concerns:第2厚生定理が意味する公平性への含意は？

A Cannot be addressed without destroying efficiency効率性を破壊せずには対処できない
B Are irrelevant to economic analysis経済分析と無関係
C Are automatically solved by competitive markets競争市場によって自動的に解決される
D Can in principle be separated from efficiency — redistribute endowments, then let markets work原理的に効率性と分離可能 — 初期賦存量を再分配し、その後市場に委ねる
Separate equity from efficiency (in principle)原理的に効率性と分離可能 — The 2nd theorem says: choose the desired distribution, redistribute initial endowments as lump sums, then let competitive markets achieve Pareto optimality at that distribution. Equity and efficiency can theoretically coexist.第2定理は「望ましい分配を選択し、初期賦存量を一括移転した後、競争市場にパレート最適を達成させる」ことを示す。公平性と効率性は理論上共存できる。

Q7 Point J is inside the Edgeworth box but off the contract curve. This means:点JはEdgeworth box内だが契約曲線外にある。これは何を意味するか？

A J is Pareto optimalJはPareto最適
B J maximizes social welfareJは社会厚生を最大化する
C There exist reallocations that can make at least one party better off without harming the other一方を悪化させずに他方を改善できる再配分が存在する
D J is outside the feasible setJは実現可能集合の外にある
Pareto improvement is possible from JJからPareto改善が可能 — Off the contract curve, the lens-shaped region between the two tangent IQ (or IC) curves at J contains all allocations that are better for at least one party. Moving to the contract curve within this lens is a Pareto improvement.契約曲線外の点Jでは、2つのIQまたはIC曲線の間のレンズ形領域内に少なくとも一方にとってより良い配分が存在。このレンズ内で契約曲線に移動することがPareto改善。

Q8 The Marginal Rate of Transformation (MRTXY) equals:限界変換率（MRTXY）は何に等しいか？

A MUX / MUY
B MPL / MPK
C MCX / MCY
D PX / PY
MCX/MCY — MRTXY is the slope of the Production Possibilities Frontier (PPF). It represents the opportunity cost of producing one more unit of X in terms of Y foregone. Since producing more X requires reallocating resources, MRT = MCX/MCY.MRTXYは生産可能性フロンティア（PPF）の傾き。Xをもう1単位生産する機会費用をY単位で表したもの。Xを増産するには資源を再配分する必要があるため、MRT = MCX/MCY。

Q9 The Production Possibilities Frontier (PPF) is typically concave (bowed outward). This is because:生産可能性フロンティア（PPF）は通常、原点から外側に凸（凹型）になる。その理由は？

A All resources are identical and equally suitable for both goodsすべての資源が同一でどちらの財にも同等に適している
B Increasing opportunity cost — as more X is produced, each additional unit requires giving up increasingly more Y機会費用逓増 — Xを増産するにつれ、追加の1単位ごとにより多くのYを犠牲にする必要がある
C Decreasing returns to scale in all productionあらゆる生産における規模収益逓減
D The Pareto condition MRS = MRT must holdパレート条件MRS = MRTが成立する必要があるため
Increasing opportunity cost機会費用逓増 — Resources are not perfectly interchangeable between goods. As we shift more resources toward X, we must use inputs that are increasingly suited to Y — making each additional unit of X more costly in terms of Y. This gives the PPF its concave shape.資源は財間で完全に代替可能ではない。X生産に資源をシフトするにつれ、Yに適した投入物を使わざるを得ず、XのY単位での機会費用が逓増する。これがPPFの凹型の形状の理由。

Q10 The First Welfare Theorem (competitive equilibrium → Pareto optimality) holds under which key assumption?第1厚生定理（競争均衡→パレート最適）が成立する重要な仮定は？

A The government provides all public goods政府がすべての公共財を供給する
B All individuals have the same utility functionすべての個人が同じ効用関数を持つ
C Income is distributed equally所得が均等に分配されている
D No externalities, no public goods, no asymmetric information — i.e. perfect competition conditions hold外部性・公共財・情報の非対称性がない — 完全競争の条件が成立
Perfect competition conditions hold完全競争の条件が成立 — The First Welfare Theorem requires conditions for perfect competition: no market power, no externalities, no public goods, no asymmetric information, and complete markets. When any of these fails — i.e. market failure occurs — the theorem breaks down and government intervention may be justified.第1厚生定理は完全競争の条件を要求：市場支配力なし・外部性なし・公共財なし・情報の非対称性なし・完全な市場。これらのいずれかが失敗する場合（=市場の失敗）、定理は成立せず政府介入が正当化されうる。
